For a one-day visit to **Death Valley National Park**, with a balance of **hiking, scenic drives, and safety**, here’s a solid itinerary:

### **Morning (Early Start: 7-8 AM)**

#### **1. Zabriskie Point (Sunrise or Early Visit)**

- Short **0.3-mile walk** from the parking lot to an iconic viewpoint.
- Stunning golden badlands scenery.
- Best in the morning when the sun lights up the landscape.

#### **2. Golden Canyon & Red Cathedral Hike**

- **Distance:** 3 miles round trip.
- **Difficulty:** Moderate.
- **Highlights:** Beautiful canyon walls, potential extension to Red Cathedral.
- **Safety Tip:** Start early to avoid extreme heat, carry plenty of water.

### **Mid-Morning (9-11 AM)**

#### **3. Badwater Basin**

- Lowest point in North America (-282 ft).
- Unique salt flats and vast open space.
- Short walk to get the full experience.

#### **4. Devil’s Golf Course (Quick Stop)**

- A bizarre field of jagged salt formations.
- Short detour, no hiking required.

### **Midday (11 AM - 1 PM)**

#### **5. Artist’s Drive & Artist’s Palette**

- A 9-mile scenic loop (one-way).
- Stunning colorful hills due to mineral deposits.
- Stop at **Artist’s Palette viewpoint** for a short walk.

### **Lunch Break (1-2 PM)**

- **Furnace Creek Visitor Center**: Learn about the park, refill water.
- **Grab lunch at The Ranch at Death Valley** or bring your own picnic.

### **Afternoon (2-5 PM)**

#### **6. Mesquite Flat Sand Dunes**

- Iconic sand dunes with great photo ops.
- Walk as far as you like, but be mindful of the heat.
- Best enjoyed in the late afternoon.

#### **7. Dante’s View (Best for Sunset)**

- **Drive-up viewpoint at 5,476 ft.**
- Offers a **stunning panoramic view** over Death Valley.
- Cooler temperatures due to elevation.

### **Safety Tips for Solo Travel**

- **Start early** to avoid midday heat.
- **Bring at least 1 gallon of water** (more if hiking).
- **Download offline maps**, as cell service is limited.
- **Let someone know your itinerary**.
- **Stay on marked trails** and avoid slot canyons if there’s any chance of rain.

Yes! You'll be **driving between all the spots**, as Death Valley is **huge** and distances between locations can be significant. Here's a breakdown of approximate driving times:

- **Zabriskie Point → Golden Canyon** (~5 min)
- **Golden Canyon → Badwater Basin** (~20 min)
- **Badwater Basin → Devil’s Golf Course** (~10 min)
- **Devil’s Golf Course → Artist’s Drive (loop)** (~25 min including stops)
- **Artist’s Drive → Furnace Creek Visitor Center (lunch stop)** (~15 min)
- **Furnace Creek → Mesquite Flat Sand Dunes** (~30 min)
- **Mesquite Flat Sand Dunes → Dante’s View** (~50 min, but worth it!)
