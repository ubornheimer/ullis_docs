# Summary

Ultimate frisbee, usually just called **ultimate**, is a **team field sport played with a flying disc**. Two teams try to score by passing the disc to a teammate who catches it in the end zone they are attacking. The thrower may not run with the disc, but can pass in any direction; an incomplete pass, interception, drop, or out-of-bounds pass is a turnover.

In standard outdoor ultimate, teams usually play **7 vs 7** on a rectangular field with end zones, though beach, indoor, mixed, and smaller-sided formats are common. USA Ultimate describes the sport as **non-contact** and **self-officiated**, with players responsible for their own foul and line calls.

What makes ultimate distinctive is **Spirit of the Game**: competitive play is encouraged, but players are expected to know the rules, be honest, avoid intentional fouls, and resolve disputes fairly. WFDF’s rules state that ultimate relies on every player taking responsibility for fair play; many tournaments also use spirit scoring after games.

Strategically, ultimate combines elements of football, basketball, and netball: the offense uses cutting, spacing, resets, and continuation passes to move the disc; the defense tries to force difficult throws, deny open cuts, and create turnovers. Because the disc can curve, float, and be thrown in many ways, throwing skill and timing are central to the sport.
